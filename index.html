How the different protocols stack up

The main protocols were found were:

  • TCN
  • PrivateKit SafePaths
  • BlueTrace/OpenTrace
  • PEPP-PT
  • DP3T

Of these, three distinct groups arise: Decentralized geolocation (Private Kit SafePaths), decentralized Bluetooth (DP^3T, TCN, Apple/Google), and centralized Bluetooth (PEPP-PT, ROBERT, OpenTrace). No protocols were found to conduct centralized geolocation-based contact-tracing, since this is essentially government surveillance.

By "centralized", we mean that the procotol does one or more of the following: (a) stores the pseudonymous IDs of each user on a central server; (b) conducts the contact matching process via a central server. Conversely, decentralized protocols require the application to store all data and conduct all contact matching on user devices, minimizing the opportunity for a a central authority to re-identify users or control their data.
